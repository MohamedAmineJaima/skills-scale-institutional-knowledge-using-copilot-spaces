## Purpose
Define the initial steps to validate and authorize work, align stakeholders, and create a lightweight plan.

## When to use
Whenever a new project idea or feature proposal is ready to be explored.

## Goals
- Confirm business need and measurable outcome
- Identify stakeholders & champions
- Define success criteria and initial timeline
- Decide go/no-go for planning

## Minimum Deliverables
- Project One-pager (Problem, Goal, Success Metrics)
- Stakeholder list & communication plan
- High-level timeline and key milestones
- Initial risk list
- Resource needs (team roles, rough effort estimate)
- Initial requirements documentation (Business Analyst)
- Preliminary success metrics and data tracking plan (Data Analyst)

## Initiation Checklist
- [ ] One-pager completed and reviewed by Product Lead
- [ ] Sponsor / Stakeholder alignment (email or meeting)
- [ ] Initial requirements gathering completed (see Business Analyst checklist below)
- [ ] Decision: Approve to move into planning?
- [ ] Create repo or project board skeleton
- [ ] Add initial artifacts to repo (docs/ or .copilot/)

## Business Analyst: Requirements Gathering Checklist

The **Business Analyst** leads requirements gathering during project initiation to ensure clear understanding of business needs.

### Pre-Kickoff Activities
- [ ] Identify key stakeholders and business owners
- [ ] Review existing documentation (business cases, proposals, related projects)
- [ ] Prepare stakeholder interview guide with key questions
- [ ] Understand current-state processes and pain points

### Requirements Elicitation
- [ ] Conduct stakeholder interviews to gather business needs
- [ ] Facilitate requirements workshop with cross-functional participants
- [ ] Document business objectives and success criteria
- [ ] Identify regulatory, compliance, or policy constraints
- [ ] Capture integration requirements with existing systems

### Requirements Documentation
- [ ] Create high-level use cases or user scenarios
- [ ] Document functional and non-functional requirements
- [ ] Identify data requirements and dependencies
- [ ] Document assumptions and open questions
- [ ] Prioritize requirements (must-have vs. nice-to-have)

### Requirements Validation
- [ ] Review requirements with stakeholders for accuracy and completeness
- [ ] Validate requirements alignment with business objectives
- [ ] Identify requirements gaps or conflicts
- [ ] Confirm requirements are feasible and testable
- [ ] Obtain stakeholder sign-off on documented requirements

### Handoff to Planning
- [ ] Package requirements documentation for planning phase
- [ ] Brief Product Manager and Project Manager on key findings
- [ ] Flag high-risk or complex requirements for early attention
- [ ] Prepare to support backlog refinement and story creation

## Role Involvement in Initiation

- **Product Manager**: Owns the project one-pager, defines success metrics, aligns with stakeholders
- **Project Manager**: Identifies resource needs, creates initial timeline, facilitates kickoff
- **Business Analyst**: Leads requirements gathering, documents business needs, validates with stakeholders
- **Data Analyst**: Defines tracking requirements and instrumentation for success metrics
- **QA Lead**: Reviews requirements for testability, flags quality concerns early
- **UX Designer**: Conducts initial user research if needed, identifies UX considerations

## Decision Gate
Move to planning when:
- Success metrics are clear and tracking plan is defined
- Business requirements are documented and validated
- Stakeholders agree on priority and scope
- Team availability is confirmed
- No blocking risks or dependencies identified
