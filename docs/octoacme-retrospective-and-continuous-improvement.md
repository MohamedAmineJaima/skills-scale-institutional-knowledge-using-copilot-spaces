# OctoAcme — Retrospective & Continuous Improvement

## Purpose
Capture learnings and convert them into actionable improvements.

## When
After each sprint, release, or important milestone. Also after incidents.

## Structure
- What went well
- What could be improved
- Action items (owner, due date)
- Follow-up on previous action items

## Running a Retrospective
- Timebox: 45–75 minutes depending on team size
- Use an anonymous idea board if needed to encourage candor
- Prioritize 2–3 top action items to avoid overload
- Scrum Master facilitates and ensures psychological safety
- Include all roles: Developers, QA Lead, UX Designer, Business Analyst, Data Analyst, Product Manager, Project Manager

## Tracking Improvements
- Add action items to the project backlog or issues with clear owners and timelines
- Review outstanding actions in the weekly PM sync
- Scrum Master tracks action item completion
- Data Analyst measures impact of process improvements where applicable

## Role Involvement in Retrospectives

- **Scrum Master**: Facilitates retrospective, creates safe space for feedback, tracks action items
- **Developers**: Share technical challenges, suggest process improvements
- **QA Lead**: Provide insights on quality issues, testing bottlenecks, defect patterns
- **UX Designer**: Share design iteration learnings, usability test findings
- **Business Analyst**: Reflect on requirements clarity, stakeholder alignment issues
- **Data Analyst**: Share metric insights, data about team performance and outcomes
- **Product Manager**: Provide product context, prioritize improvement actions
- **Project Manager**: Document action items, track improvement implementation
